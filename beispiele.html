I was on the Ringbahn in Berlin on my way back from Ikea, about a year ago,…

and I’d bought a plant and i was standing in the train carriage, with the plant, about 3 foot tall, on my way home and I saw at the other end of the carriage a man staring right at me. So immediately I thought ‘What is happening here? Why is he looking at me? Where do I look?’ This man looked, I guess he was around 50, looked like some very typical Berliner; a beer in his hand, it was the afternoon and I was just standing there thinking “Why is he looking at me?” And then slowly he started coming towards me, up through the middle of the carriage, still looking at me. Again in my head thinking ‘What’s happening?… Uh-oh.’ I felt uncomfortable. And then he came right up close to me and just sort of whispered almost in my ear and said “Don’t water that too much; it doesn’t need much water.” And then the train stopped, the doors opened, he got out and he was gone. And I was left standing there. It was sort of one of those wonderful feelings of sort of shock, of having this idea about something or someone who I don’t know at all and being wrong. It’s somehow a really nice feeling to get to be wrong about some impression you have of someone. And I still have the plant. It’s alive. I haven’t watered it too much but, also, I’ve never been that good at watering plants. But I do think of him very time that I do occasionally water the plant.
